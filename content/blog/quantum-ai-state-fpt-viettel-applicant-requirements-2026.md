# Quantum AI in 2026: Technical Reality, and What FPT & Viettel Want in Applicants

**Date:** 2026-04-20  
**Tags:** quantum-ai, vietnam-tech, careers, fpt, viettel

---

Quantum AI moved from “hype vocabulary” to “engineering workload” over the period from **Dec 2025 to Apr 2026**. The biggest shift is not that quantum computers suddenly became fault-tolerant; they did not. The shift is that AI is increasingly used as an operational layer for quantum systems: calibration, error decoding, and workflow automation.

For Vietnam, the period is equally important. FPT publicly scaled its strategic investment in Quantum AI, and Viettel’s hiring signals showed demand for people who can operate across quantum theory, software tooling, and security applications.

This post has two goals:
1. Give a technical snapshot of Quantum AI’s current state (Dec 2025–Apr 2026).
2. Decode what **FPT** and **Viettel** are actually looking for in quantum applicants.

---

## 1) Current State of Quantum AI (Dec 2025 → Apr 2026)

## 1.1 The center of gravity: AI-for-quantum operations

One of the strongest industry signals in this window is NVIDIA’s April 2026 launch of **Ising**, described as open AI models for quantum calibration and error correction workflows. The practical message is clear: a large part of “Quantum AI” today is not quantum replacing AI, but AI helping quantum hardware become usable.

That aligns with where most quantum systems still are: noisy, limited-qubit, and difficult to run reproducibly without strong classical control loops.

## 1.2 Hardware access is widening, but still selective

In April 2026, coverage from Quantum Computing Report highlighted Google Quantum AI accepting proposals for early access to the Willow processor. This indicates two things:
- Quantum hardware access is expanding beyond internal teams.
- The bar remains high: teams must propose rigorous, high-impact experiments.

So the ecosystem is opening, but not yet “developer commodity.”

## 1.3 What this means technically

By April 2026, a realistic technical framing is:
- **Near-term value = hybrid workflows** (classical HPC/AI + quantum experiments).
- **Key bottlenecks = calibration, error correction, and algorithm-to-hardware fit**.
- **Best opportunities = teams that bridge theory, software tooling, and domain use cases**.

In short: Quantum AI is now an engineering discipline with measurable constraints, not a purely speculative frontier.

## 2) Vietnam Signal: FPT and Viettel Are Building Different Quantum Profiles

## 2.1 FPT signal (institution-building + applied R&D)

In Dec 2025, public reporting and FPT communication signaled a major move: formation of the **Quantum AI & Cyber Security Institute (QACI)** and a stated **USD 100M investment** direction. By Mar 2026, FPT–PTIT collaboration messaging emphasized:
- doctoral-level talent pipelines,
- co-supervised research tied to practical problems,
- and outputs aimed at deployable, potentially commercializable technologies.

This points to a long-horizon model: **research depth + real-world transfer**.

## 2.2 Viettel signal (product-oriented quantum engineering)

Publicly indexed Viettel/Viettel High Tech hiring content (Jan–Apr 2026) emphasizes hands-on roles: quantum algorithms, quantum photonics, and quantum-security-adjacent engineering. Requirements repeatedly center on:
- concrete algorithmic competency,
- software stack readiness,
- and implementation constraints in noisy systems.

This points to a faster application loop: **R&D that can ship into mission/industry contexts**.

---

## 3) What FPT Seems to Require in Quantum Applicants

From Dec 2025–Apr 2026 public signals, FPT appears to prioritize these applicant traits:

1. **Research-grade foundation**
   - Strong advanced training track (Master/PhD trajectory preferred in many contexts).
   - Ability to produce formal outputs: papers, IP, technical reports.

2. **Applied research mindset**
   - Not research for research’s sake.
   - Capacity to map quantum/AI methods to practical national or enterprise problems.

3. **Cross-institution collaboration ability**
   - Comfortable working across university labs, enterprise R&D units, and interdisciplinary teams.
   - Able to co-mentor or contribute in structured talent programs.

4. **Long-horizon commitment**
   - FPT’s messaging around training pipelines and strategic technologies suggests preference for people who can build capability over years, not only short-term experimentation.

**Bottom line:** FPT appears to value candidates who can operate at the intersection of **deep research, talent development, and eventual productization**.

---

## 4) What Viettel Seems to Require in Quantum Applicants

From public job descriptions and indexed postings in the target period, Viettel-oriented roles commonly ask for:

1. **Core quantum + math fundamentals**
   - Quantum mechanics basics (qubits, superposition, entanglement, Hilbert-space reasoning).
   - Strong linear algebra, probability, optimization.

2. **Quantum software/tooling readiness**
   - Familiarity with frameworks such as **Qiskit, Cirq, PennyLane, Braket, QuTiP**.
   - Ability to simulate, benchmark, and optimize circuits under NISQ constraints.

3. **Engineering execution in noisy reality**
   - Circuit optimization, error mitigation, performance evaluation before hardware deployment.
   - Practical coding competence (Python-first; systems optimization is a plus).

4. **Security-aware quantum application thinking**
   - Interest/experience in **QRNG/QKD/PQC** and post-processing pipelines.
   - Awareness of the cryptography transition implications.

5. **Interdisciplinary collaboration**
   - Working across physics, electronics, cryptography, and software teams.
   - Communicating technical trade-offs to mixed audiences.

**Bottom line:** Viettel appears to prefer applicants who can convert quantum theory into **working pipelines and deployable engineering outcomes**.

If you want to match both profiles (deep R&D + engineering delivery), here is a focused plan:

## Days 1–30: Foundations + one reproducible notebook
- Review quantum mechanics for computing (states, operators, measurement).
- Re-implement core algorithms (Deutsch-Jozsa, Grover, QFT).
- Deliverable: one clean notebook with experiments, plots, and interpretation.

## Days 31–60: Toolchain + hardware constraints
- Use Qiskit/Cirq/PennyLane on simulators.
- Practice transpilation, circuit depth reduction, and basic error mitigation.
- Deliverable: benchmark report comparing at least two algorithm variants under noise.

## Days 61–90: Security or domain capstone
- Build one mini-project: e.g., QKD post-processing simulation, QRNG entropy analysis, or optimization prototype.
- Write a short technical report as if for an internal R&D review.
- Deliverable: public repo + 4–6 page report + 1-page executive summary.

This gives you evidence for both hiring lenses:
- **FPT lens:** research rigor and structured output.
- **Viettel lens:** implementability, performance thinking, and mission relevance.

---

## 6) Final Take

Between Dec 2025 and Apr 2026, Quantum AI became more operational and less rhetorical. The strongest global signal is AI improving quantum system reliability. The strongest Vietnam signal is capability-building by major players, but with different emphasis:

- **FPT:** institution-scale R&D, talent pipelines, and strategic long-term depth.
- **Viettel:** engineering-ready quantum talent for applied, security-aware systems.

For applicants, the winning profile is no longer “I know quantum theory.” It is: **I can prove quantum understanding through reproducible software, measurable performance, and real problem framing.**
